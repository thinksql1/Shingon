Lesson 2: The Core Teachings of Mikkyō

Dainichi Nyorai: The Central Deity

[span_36](start_span)

At the very center of the Shingon pantheon is Dainichi Nyorai (Skt. Mahāvairocana), the "Great Sun" Buddha[span_36](end_span). [span_37](start_span)Unlike the historical Shakyamuni Buddha, Dainichi Nyorai is not a historical figure but the cosmic, all-encompassing Buddha who is the source of all phenomena in the universe[span_37](end_span). [span_38](start_span)Shingon teachings hold that this central Buddha is the source of many different Buddha-manifestations, each an independent deity in its own right[span_38](end_span). [span_39](start_span)[span_40](start_span)The goal of Shingon practice is to realize one's fundamental identity with this universal Buddha "in this very body"[span_39](end_span)[span_40](end_span).

The Three Secret Activities of Body, Speech, and Mind

[span_41](start_span)

Shingon practice is based on the "Three Mysteries" or "Three Secrets" (Sammitsu), which are the activities of body, speech, and mind[span_41](end_span). [span_42](start_span)The esoteric teachings systematize these activities and present them as inseparable aspects of a symbolic practice leading to the rapid attainment of enlightenment[span_42](end_span). [span_43](start_span)The practitioner seeks to identify their own actions, words, and thoughts with those of the Buddha[span_43](end_span).

The Secret of Body: Esoteric Mudra

[span_44](start_span)

The secret activity of the body involves the use of mudras, which are symbolic hand gestures[span_44](end_span). [span_45](start_span)[span_46](start_span)These prescribed postures are not merely symbolic but are believed to form a spiritual connection with the deities and invoke their power, aligning the practitioner's physical form with that of the Buddha[span_45](end_span)[span_46](end_span).

The Secret of Speech: Esoteric Mantra Practice

[span_47](start_span)

The secret activity of speech is the chanting of mantras[span_47](end_span). [span_48](start_span)The term "Shingon" itself means "True Word," referring to these powerful incantations[span_48](end_span). [span_49](start_span)[span_50](start_span)Mantras are not simple prayers but are considered the sacred sounds and syllables that embody the essence of the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, unifying the practitioner's speech with the cosmic truth[span_49](end_span)[span_50](end_span).

The Secret of Mind: Internal Visualization

[span_51](start_span)[span_52](start_span)

The secret activity of mind is the practice of internal visualization, often referred to as meditation (samadhi)[span_51](end_span)[span_52](end_span). [span_53](start_span)The practitioner visualizes deities, Sanskrit syllables, and mandalas in their mind's eye[span_53](end_span). [span_54](start_span)[span_55](start_span)This practice is designed to transform ordinary consciousness into wisdom by focusing the mind and uniting it with the enlightened mind of the Buddha[span_54](end_span)[span_55](end_span).

The Dynamic Mandala

[span_56](start_span)

The mandala is a core component of Mikkyō ritual and a graphic illustration of the esoteric cosmos[span_56](end_span). [span_57](start_span)Shingon uses a dual mandala system, representing two fundamental aspects of the cosmic truth[span_57](end_span).

  • The Womb World Mandala (Taizōkai): Based on the Dainichi-kyō, this mandala represents the world of principle and compassion. [span_58](start_span)[span_59](start_span)It is oriented to the east and depicts Dainichi Buddha at the center of an eight-petaled lotus, surrounded by 409 deities in twelve rectangular "halls"[span_58](end_span)[span_59](end_span).
  • The Diamond World Mandala (Kongōkai): Based on the Kongōchō-gyō, this mandala represents the world of wisdom. [span_60](start_span)[span_61](start_span)It is oriented to the west and is composed of nine distinct square assemblies, containing a total of 1461 deities, depicting the indestructible nature of enlightenment[span_60](end_span)[span_61](end_span).
[span_62](start_span)

These are not just art; they are tools for meditation and ritual, helping the practitioner visualize the entire pantheon and their relationship to the central Buddha[span_62](end_span).
